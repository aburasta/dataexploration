# Akan Spirituality Skool Community — Launch Playbook

*A step-by-step plan for building a Skool community that uses the tenets of Akan spirituality (Ghana) to guide people on how to live well.*

---

## 1. Vision & Positioning

### The niche statement

> **"Ancient Akan wisdom for modern living."** A community for people of African descent (and anyone drawn to African spiritual systems) who want to live with purpose, honor their ancestors, and build good character — guided by the spiritual worldview of the Akan people of Ghana.

You are not selling religion. You are selling a **life operating system**: purpose (nkrabea), character (suban), ancestral connection (Nsamanfo), and community (the village) — drawn from one of the world's most coherent and beautiful spiritual traditions.

### Who it's for

Primary avatars:

1. **The Diaspora Seeker** — African American / Afro-Caribbean / Black British person, 25–50, exploring spirituality beyond the church, wants connection to African roots but doesn't know where to start. Often found in "ancestral healing," "decolonize your spirituality," and DNA-ancestry spaces.
2. **The Burnt-Out Striver** — successful on paper, spiritually empty, looking for purpose and grounding practices that aren't generic Western self-help.
3. **The Heritage Reclaimer** — Ghanaian/West African heritage, raised away from tradition, wants to understand what their grandparents practiced.

### Your positioning: the Learner-Guide (critical)

You are a **passionate learner, not an initiated priest (ɔkɔmfo)**. This is not a weakness — it's your positioning. Frame the community as:

> *"I'm a few steps ahead on the path, walking it with you — not a guru at the top of the mountain."*

This positioning is actually **easier to market** (people trust fellow travelers more than self-proclaimed gurus), protects you ethically, and gives the community a built-in growth story: members literally learn alongside you.

### The Ethics & Cultural Respect Charter

Publish this in the community from day one. It builds trust and protects you:

- **What we do:** Study Akan cosmology, proverbs, symbols, and values; apply them as life philosophy; practice ancestor reverence and reflection; credit and cite Ghanaian scholars and practitioners.
- **What we don't do:** Perform or teach initiations, claim priesthood, sell divination or "spiritual services," diagnose spiritual problems, or present this as a substitute for medical/mental-health care.
- **Sourcing:** Lessons draw on credited sources — e.g. Kofi Asare Opoku (*West African Traditional Religion*), J.B. Danquah (*The Akan Doctrine of God*), Kwame Gyekye (*An Essay on African Philosophical Thought*), Peggy Appiah & Kwame Anthony Appiah (*Bu Me Bɛ: Proverbs of the Akans*), Anthony Ephirim-Donkor (*African Spirituality*).
- **Elders first:** As the community grows, revenue funds honoraria for Ghanaian practitioners, scholars, and elders to teach live — the community becomes a bridge to the source, not a replacement for it.
- **Sacred boundaries:** Some knowledge in Akan tradition is reserved for initiates. We respect that line and say so openly.

### Name ideas for the community

- **Sankofa Circle** — most marketable; Sankofa is recognizable to the diaspora
- **The Akan Way**
- **Kra & Character** (Kra = soul)
- **Nyansa** (Twi for "wisdom") — e.g. "Nyansa: Akan Wisdom for Modern Life"
- **The Village Fire**

Check name availability on Skool, Instagram, TikTok, and YouTube before committing.

## 2. Core Curriculum: "Akan Wisdom for Modern Life"

Structure this as Skool Classroom courses (both Skool plans include unlimited courses). Each module = one course; each lesson = a 5–15 min video + a written summary + a reflection/action prompt members post about in the community (this drives Skool's gamification engine — see §3).

### Module 0 — Start Here (onboarding course)

1. Welcome: what this community is and isn't (the Charter)
2. Who the Akan are: Ghana, the Asante and other Akan peoples, why this tradition survived and traveled (its imprint on the diaspora)
3. The map of the journey: the five paths below
4. Your first assignment: find your **Akan day name** and introduce yourself with it (see lead magnet, §5)

### Module 1 — Sankofa: Healing Your Past *(life domain: identity & healing)*

*"Se wo were fi na wosankofa a yenkyi" — it is not taboo to go back and fetch what you forgot.*

1. The Sankofa principle: the past as a resource, not a wound
2. Knowing your roots: family history, lineage mapping, naming what was lost
3. Ancestral healing: breaking cycles vs. honoring inheritance
4. Practice: writing your lineage story; a Sankofa journal ritual

### Module 2 — Kra & Nkrabea: Your Soul and Destiny *(life domain: purpose)*

1. The Akan anatomy of a person: **ɔkra** (soul, the spark of Nyame), **sunsum** (spirit/personality), **mogya** (blood/maternal line), **ntorɔ** (paternal spirit)
2. **Nkrabea** (destiny): the Akan view that you arrived with a purpose — and how that reframes career, calling, and failure
3. **Kradin** (day names): Kwasi/Akosua through Kwame/Ama — the qualities tradition associates with your birth day, used as a mirror for self-reflection
4. Practice: writing a personal destiny statement; aligning your week with your nkrabea

### Module 3 — Nyame, the Abosom & the Nsamanfo: The Spiritual Ecosystem *(life domain: spiritual practice)*

1. **Nyame/Onyankopɔn** (the Supreme Being) and **Asase Yaa** (Mother Earth) — and why the Akan say "no one shows a child the sky"
2. The **abosom** (divinities of rivers, forests, places) — nature as alive and sacred; an Akan ecology
3. The **Nsamanfo** (ancestors): why the dead are not gone, and what it means to live so your descendants will call your name
4. Reverence in practice: gratitude libation (taught as a reflective gratitude practice, not priestly ritual), an ancestor table/altar of remembrance, **Adae**-inspired remembrance days on the traditional 40-day cycle
5. Practice: a daily morning acknowledgment; a weekly ancestor reflection

### Module 4 — Adinkra & Proverbs: A Code for Daily Conduct *(life domain: ethics & decisions)*

The Akan say **a person's character (suban) is their religion**. This module is the heart of "how to live."

1. Reading Adinkra: symbols as compressed philosophy
2. **Gye Nyame** ("except God") — humility, surrender, what you control
3. **Dwennimmen** (ram's horns) — strength with humility
4. **Nkyinkyim** (twisting) — adaptability and resilience
5. **Aya** (fern) — endurance through hardship
6. **Nea Onnim No Sua A, Ohu** ("he who does not know can know from learning") — lifelong learning
7. Proverbs (ɛbɛ) as decision tools: one proverb per week applied to real member situations
8. Practice: choose your "governing symbol" for the season; proverb journaling

### Module 5 — The Village: Community, Family & Wealth *(life domain: relationships & livelihood)*

1. The communal self: "I am because we are" — the abusua (clan/family) and mutual obligation
2. Eldership and mentorship: how to be a good elder-in-training
3. Akan ethics of wealth and work: industry, generosity, and reputation
4. Conflict and repair: restorative approaches drawn from Akan custom
5. Practice: a service commitment inside the community; a "village check-in" partner system

### Drip schedule

Release Module 0 + Module 1 at launch, then one module per month. This gives you 5–6 months of programmed content and a reason for members to stay.

## 3. Skool Community Structure

### Spaces / post categories

- 🔥 **The Village Fire** — general discussion, wins, introductions
- 🌀 **Sankofa Stories** — lineage discoveries, healing journeys
- 🪞 **Proverb of the Week** — you post one proverb weekly; members apply it to their lives (your highest-engagement ritual)
- 🕯️ **Ancestor Honoring** — remembrance posts, Adae-day reflections
- 🧭 **Purpose & Destiny** — nkrabea work, accountability
- 📚 **Study Hall** — book discussions, scholar spotlights, questions
- 📣 **Announcements** — admin only

### Rules (pin these)

1. Respect the tradition — no mockery, no appropriation-for-clout
2. No selling spiritual services; no diagnosing anyone's "spiritual problems"
3. Share lived experience, not medical/legal/spiritual prescriptions
4. What's shared at the fire stays at the fire
5. Disagree like family: directly, then warmly

### Onboarding flow

New member → auto-DM welcome with 3 steps: (1) take Module 0, (2) find your day name and introduce yourself in The Village Fire using it, (3) attend the next live call. Skool shows new-member checklists well; keep it to three actions.

### Gamification → "The Growth Path"

Skool's points/levels system (members earn points when others like their posts/comments) maps naturally to a village metaphor. Rename the 9 levels, e.g.:

| Skool level | Title |
|---|---|
| 1 | Newcomer (Ɔhɔho) |
| 2 | Seeker |
| 3 | Student of the Fire |
| 4 | Keeper of Proverbs |
| 5 | Sankofa Walker |
| 6 | Village Builder |
| 7 | Wisdom Bearer |
| 8 | Elder-in-Training |
| 9 | Ɔpanyin (Elder) |

Unlock perks by level: e.g. Level 3 unlocks Module 2+, Level 5 unlocks a monthly small-group call. (Skool supports level-gated course access — this dramatically boosts engagement.)

### Weekly rhythm (your operating cadence)

- **Monday:** Proverb of the Week post (5 min to create, highest ROI)
- **Wednesday:** Live call — alternating: teaching (you), study hall (book/scholar), guest elder (as budget allows), member hot-seats
- **Friday:** "Village check-in" thread — wins, struggles, gratitude
- **Every 6th Sunday (Akwasidae-inspired):** special ancestor-honoring live gathering — your signature event, tied to the real Akan calendar

Total founder workload: ~4–6 hrs/week once the curriculum backlog exists.

## 4. Monetization: Recommendation & Math

### Recommendation: **Free → Paid in two phases**

**Phase 1 (months 0–4): Free community.** You have no audience yet; the free community *is* your audience-building engine (Skool has internal discovery, and "join free" converts cold social-media traffic far better than a paywall). Goal: 200–500 free members and proven engagement rituals.

**Phase 2 (month 4–6+): Add the paid tier at $39/month** (test $29–49). Skool lets you run the free community as a funnel into a paid one. The paid tier contains: full Classroom access beyond Module 1, all live calls + recordings, small-group circles, and guest-elder sessions. Keep the free tier alive forever as top-of-funnel (Proverb of the Week stays free — it's your ad).

Anchor offer for launch: **Founding Member** — first 30 paid members get $24/mo locked for life. Scarcity + reward for early trust.

### Platform cost (verified June 2026)

- **Skool Hobby: $9/month** + ~10% transaction fee on member payments
- **Skool Pro: $99/month** with much lower fees
- Crossover: Pro becomes cheaper at roughly **$1,267/month** in community revenue
- Both plans: unlimited members, unlimited courses, Classroom, gamification, 14-day free trial

**Start on Hobby ($9/mo).** Upgrade to Pro when paid revenue passes ~$1.3k/mo.

## 5. Audience-Building From Zero (90-day pre-launch)

You have no audience, so the community launch *is* a content-creation project first. Pick **one primary platform and one repurpose platform** — do not try to be everywhere.

**Recommended: TikTok/Instagram Reels (primary) + YouTube (repurpose/long-form).** Spirituality, ancestor-veneration, and "decolonize your faith" content performs strongly in short-form video; the diaspora audience for this niche actively searches there.

### Content pillars (rotate, 4–5 posts/week)

1. **Day-name & destiny** — "Born on a Friday? Here's what Akan tradition says about you." (Reliably viral format; everyone wants their own.)
2. **Proverb breakdowns** — one Akan proverb applied to a modern problem (money, heartbreak, burnout) in 45 seconds
3. **Adinkra symbol meanings** — visual, save-able, shareable
4. **Myth-busting & respect** — "African spirituality is not 'voodoo'," "what Hollywood gets wrong," "I'm a learner, not a priest — here's why that matters"
5. **Your journey** — what you're learning this week (the Learner-Guide story; this builds the parasocial trust that converts)

### The lead magnet

**"Find Your Akan Day Name & What It Means for Your Purpose"** — a free PDF/quiz: enter your birth date → your kradin, its traditional qualities, one matching proverb, one reflection exercise. Delivered via a simple landing page (Skool's free-community about-page can serve as this, or Carrd + email). Every video CTA points here; the thank-you page points to the free Skool community.

### 90-day cadence

- **Days 1–30:** Build the machine. Batch-record 30 short videos (one weekend of filming covers two weeks). Create the lead magnet. Set up the free Skool group (about page, rules, Module 0, categories). Post daily; reply to every comment.
- **Days 31–60:** Double down on what works. Check which pillar over-performs; make 60% of content that pillar. Open the free community publicly; mention it in every video ("link in bio — the village is open"). Start Proverb of the Week inside Skool even with 20 members.
- **Days 61–90:** Convert attention into the founding cohort. Run your first 3 live calls (free). Tease the paid tier story openly: "On [date], I'm opening 30 founding-member spots." Collect a waitlist post inside the free group.

Benchmarks to expect (not guarantees): 4–5 short videos/week for 90 days in this niche typically yields 3k–15k followers if hooks are decent; 1–3% will join a free community; 5–10% of engaged free members convert to paid at launch.

## 6. Launch Sequence (weeks 13–17)

- **Week 13 — Open cart:** Founding Member offer ($24/mo lock, 30 spots, 10 days). Announce in free group, every social post, and DMs to your 20 most-engaged free members personally.
- **Week 14 — Close cart + onboard:** Welcome call for founders; everyone takes Module 0–1; assign village check-in partners.
- **Weeks 15–17 — First 30 days of paid programming:** weekly live teaching call, Proverb of the Week, first Akwasidae-inspired Sunday gathering as the signature event. Obsess over the first cohort's experience — their testimonials are your next launch's marketing.
- **After:** keep the paid door open permanently at $39; re-run a promotional "cohort entry" moment each quarter.

## 7. Growth & Sustainability (months 6–18)

### Metrics that matter (check weekly)

- Free → paid conversion rate (target 5–10% of *engaged* free members)
- Paid monthly churn (target <8%; below 5% is excellent for $39 communities)
- % of paid members active in the last 7 days (Skool shows this; target >50%)
- Live-call attendance (the single best churn predictor)

### Levers, in order

1. **Guest elders & scholars** — once revenue allows ($500+/mo spare), pay Ghanaian practitioners and academics honoraria for monthly guest sessions. This is your moat, your credibility as a non-initiate, and the fulfillment of the Charter.
2. **Member-led circles** — promote Level 6+ members to host regional/topic circles; communities that survive are the ones the founder no longer carries alone.
3. **Annual premium offers** — a paid 6-week "Sankofa Intensive" cohort ($200–400), then eventually the flagship: a **heritage trip to Ghana** (Kumasi, Manhyia, Adinkra-printing in Ntonso, an Akwasidae festival) — high-ticket ($3–5k), high-meaning, and the ultimate proof the community is a bridge to the source.
4. **YouTube long-form** — by month 6, repurpose teachings into 10–20 min videos; YouTube compounds and brings the most committed members.

### Sustainability guardrails

- Never let content debt force you to skip the weekly rhythm — batch monthly.
- Revisit the Ethics Charter quarterly; as you grow, scrutiny grows. Welcome it publicly.
- Keep studying: your own visible progression (books read, trips taken, elders consulted) *is* the product roadmap.
