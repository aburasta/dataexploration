**A few tags still lean on thematically-adjacent substitutes rather than a literal match** (e.g. a

posters covering several abstract "campaign"/"election" beats) — all real period artifacts, just
not a portrait of the exact specific event, per the standing default-substitution rule. Flag any

**Notable exact-match finds:** a labeled 1924 trial photo naming Hitler/Ludendorff/Röhm/Frick by
name (tags 42-44, 53-photo-fallback), the real Bürgerbräukeller entrance and interior (5/32, 34-36),
the actual Nov 1928 Völkischer Beobachter front page (Q10), the Harzburg Front photo of Hitler
mid-speech (Q5's comic-bubble treatment), and the March 1933 newsreel's actual SA march through
the Brandenburg Gate (tags 98/141/151).

**Video sourcing dead ends worth remembering:** IA uploads tagged "fringe"/"deemphasize" have no
rights info and dubious provenance — don't use even when nothing else is found. NARA's and USHMM's
own catalogs both surfaced great-looking candidates that turned out copyright-restricted or
on-site-only; always check the actual rights statement on the item page, not just which archive
hosts it. Universal Newsreel identifiers with a single "-sN" suffix (e.g. `-s7`) are a **single
story excerpt**, not the full reel — only "-s1-N" / "-s1_N" range suffixes are the complete
multi-story newsreel.
