Everyone knows Hitler seized power. Almost no one knows he did it legally — through the courts, the ballot box, and the fine print of a democratic constitution. This is the full story of how it happened, from a failed 1923 beer-hall putsch to the day Germany's own leaders handed him the country.

In 1923 Adolf Hitler tried to take Bavaria by force and failed — sixteen of his men dead, himself in prison. It should have ended him. Instead he learned the lesson that would make him Chancellor a decade later: don't overthrow the state, get invited into it. This documentary traces the whole machine — the "stab-in-the-back" lie that fueled the movement, a compromised justice system that turned his treason trial into a national stage, the shadow party-state he built while waiting, the Wall Street Crash that finally opened the door, the industrialists who bankrolled him, and the vain, scheming conservatives — Papen, Schleicher, Hindenburg's inner circle — who each thought they could use him and control him.
