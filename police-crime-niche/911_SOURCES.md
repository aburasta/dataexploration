## 2. Where the RAW original audio lives

For your own Shorts you want first-generation audio, not other creators' edits. It comes from five places:

### a) DA / police department evidence releases (the gold standard)
- **Chris Watts**: the Weld County DA released the *entire* case file — 3 TB including all 911 audio, interrogation video, and bodycam. Request form at [weldda.com → Information on Watts Case](https://www.weldda.com/news_room/information_on_watts_case) (email dainfo@weldgov.com under Colorado Open Records Act).
- Many PDs post released 911 audio directly to their own YouTube/press pages after a case closes (e.g. the [Palm Bay PD teen-confession release](https://www.youtube.com/shorts/AZRnUtJx6JE)). Search formula: `"[case name]" 911 call released site:youtube.com` — "released" is the keyword that surfaces official audio.

### b) Public-records requests (make your own exclusives)
- 911 audio is a public record in most US states; about a dozen states treat it as confidential. State-by-state rules: [RCFP open-government guide, 911 tapes section](https://www.rcfp.org/open-government-sections/3-911-tapes/) and [LegalClarity state overview](https://legalclarity.org/are-911-calls-public-record-and-how-can-you-access-them/).
- How-to walkthrough: [FOIAfile guide to requesting 911 recordings](https://www.foiafile.com/blog/how-to-request-911-call-recordings). Example agency portals: [Fairfax County](https://www.fairfaxcounty.gov/911/records), [Mass.gov](https://www.mass.gov/how-to/request-9-1-1-recordings).
- **Critical caveat: many agencies keep recordings only 30–90 days** — request fast on fresh cases; closed-case audio comes from the DA file instead.

### c) News-organization uploads (already-cleared audio)
NBC/ABC/local affiliates upload released 911 audio within hours (e.g. [NBC's Idaho call upload](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kmsdWx0Jvzc), [ABC's kid-hero call](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=T3RDAMLeXH4)). [Law&Crime Network](https://www.youtube.com/@lawandcrimebodycam) systematically pairs 911 audio with bodycam. Local-affiliate uploads are the most complete source for small-market cases.

### d) Archives
- [Internet Archive: Sandy Hook 911 calls](https://archive.org/details/SandyHook911Calls) (court-ordered release) and the [9/11 dispatch-call collection](https://archive.org/details/911-911-disc14) — historically significant; handle with extreme care / mostly not Shorts material.
- [Broadcastify archives](https://www.broadcastify.com/archives/): police *dispatch radio* (not the 911 line itself) archived 365 days in 30-min blocks, Creative Commons licensed — excellent for authentic dispatch-chatter b-roll under narration.

### e) Court exhibits
Calls played at trial enter the public record and get uploaded by trial-coverage channels (e.g. [Cindy Anthony's calls played for the jury](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2rPu6XiPs10)). Search formula: `"[defendant]" 911 call played court/jury`.

## 3. Shorts production notes for 911 content

- Audio-first content needs a visual layer: animated waveform + word-by-word captions + case photos. The caption reveal *is* the retention mechanic.
- The strongest 911 ranking axes: "calls where the caller was the killer", "calls that solved the case", "calmest kids ever on 911", "the moment the dispatcher realized".
- Ethics/monetization: never use calls involving children as victims for shock content (kid-*hero* calls are fine and advertiser-friendly); avoid active-shooter audio (age-restriction + policy risk); prefer cases with convictions (facts settled).
